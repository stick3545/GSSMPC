The Process of Building a Computer

The process of building a computer is actually fairly simple, and there are many guides online showing the steps in detail. The one that I used the first time I built a computer, and my personal favorite, is the POV guide from Linus Tech Tips:

  1. The first step is to take out the motherboard, and place it on an anti-static workplace, such as the bag it came in, or the box.
  2. Next, lift up the lever and insert the CPU, making sure to properly align it with the socket, then lower the lever until it latches.
  3. Most coolers included with CPUs already have thermal paste on them, but third party coolers do not. If the cooler does not already have thermal paste, apply it.
  4. Insert the RAM sticks into the DIMMs on the motherboard, align the notch in the contacts with the peg in the slot. Apply firm pressure.
  5. If an M.2 SSD is present, insert it and screw it in.
  6. Insert the rear I/O shield, bend the fins back, the insert and screw in the motherboard.
  7. Insert any other drives, and screw them in.
  8. Insert any PCIE expansion cards.
  9. Insert and Secure power supply.
  10. Connect all power, data, fan, and cooling cables.
  11. You're done!
